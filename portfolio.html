Chrome Extension

As a senior project, myself and a team of 3 others built a chrome extension. The class focused on human centered design, and projects had to include a "Not You" component, meaning the project had to have end users who differ significantly from us as the developers. We chose to create a time management tool for individuals with ADHD, or other attention disorders. On countdown, chrome tabs could automatically close themselves, ending potential hyper-focus, a common trait in individuals with ADHD. Over a semester, we covered every step in the project cycle. From initial concepts, to writing Gantt charts, interviewing mock users ith a prototyped product, building, testing, the works. We whiteboarded ideas, features, flex-goals, and ultimately, a clean UI to produce an extension with good UX.

This project was written in HTML, CSS, and primarily JavaScript. (Which if you couldn't tell, Sam I snuck a little JS into this portfolio as well!) I gained experience creating chrome extensions, and working with various APIs. I have improved my ability to work in a team, as well as how to design for users who differ from myself.

Pantry Tracker (In Progress)

Well Sam this brings us up to the present! After graduating, I decided I wanted to continue to learn new languages and take on new challenges to keep myself fresh. My current personal project is an iOS app that allows you to keep track of food in your pantry, fridge, cubbords, etc.

Users can either take a picture of an individual item's barcode, or, take a picture of their whole receipt and the app will detect the UPC codes printed next to each item, using iOS's Vision and Tesseract. These barcodes are then sent to a barcode API, nutrionix, which returns the products name and image. This information is then used to create customized lists of the users fridge, pantry, etc. Items can be easily added to shopping list and viewed while away from home as a reminder of what to pick up from the store.

This project is written in swift, and marks my first experience with the language, with only a udemy tutorial to learn the syntax and xcode basics. A special thanks goes to my girlfriend for letting me use her Mac because running xcode in a virtual mac was simply painful and I never would have even gotten the project created before it crashed.
