This page just exists to tell you a little bit about myself, as well as what I've been up to the last few years. It was also a chance for me to learn and implement some neat JS to make the timeline. I hope you can tell that I have fun doing this.

High School Graduation

June 2017

Through high school, I worked full time summers and part time school years at a pizzeria to help save money for college. After I graduated from Chelsea High School I had no career plans, all I knew was I was attending the University of Michigan in the fall.

First Year

2017-2018

My first semester, I took 17 credits of wildly different classes, attempting to find something that really interested me. The problem was, everything interested me. I had a really hard time finding one subject that interested me more than all the others. Thankfully in my first year I took my first Computer Science course. I knew immediately that this was going to be my major. The next hard part was finding a specialization.
I started working at the dining hall as a way to continue having a source of income to help pay for school. I would end up becoming a manager here and working until I graduated.

Second Year

2018-2019

Knowing that I wanted to do Computer Science, I began taking pre-requisites for the major. This is the year where took my first data structures class, and really started learning c++, which all of my classes were in. I also decided in my second year that French was something that I really enjoyed learning, and wanted to make a life skill, so I decided to make it my minor in hopes of becoming as fluent as possible.

Third Year

2019-2020

This was the first year that I was really able to start exploring the different classes available to me. I took an advanced algorithms and data structures class, a computer organization class, and a class on the foundations of computer science. At this point I had only taken classes in c++, so taking classes that explored how computers operate, writing code in assembly and how the linker operates and how it translates the c++ code I'm used to writing into machine readable code, and how pipelines of instructions work to execute that assembly... I know that I'm rambling, but this was the year that I really confirmed that initial decision to pursue Computer Science.
The more I knew, the more I knew that I didn't know. There is a continually receding horizon of knowledge in Computer Science, and all of it is fascinating to me.

Forth Year

2020-2021

In my last year, I took several different classes, still searching for one field that eclipsed all the others as my favorite. I took Security, Operating Systems, Artifical Intelligence, Web Systems, and finally Human Centered SW design. While I enjoyed all of my classes, and the opportunites to learn Python, HTML, JavaScript, and a passable amount of Go, designing Web Systems was certainly the most fascinating to me, albeit not the most intuitive. I worked hard, and I learned a lot. Learning to create client side and server side dynamic pages, and host them on Amazon W3 was immensely rewarding.
I am also incredibly glad I took a class on human centered software design, because of the soft skills learned. Using Gantt Charts, the agile methodology, taking a project from initial storyboarding to user testing and demonstration, and many other skills.

Current

2021-Present

After graduating, I moved to Colorado with my girlfriend to begin pursuing full time work, while also enjoying the beautiful state. We have visited 4 national parks so far, and several other state and county parks.
I am currently working on an app as a means of furthering my portfolio as well as my skills. This is my first app, written in Swift which I am learning as I go. My future plans include creating another web site to further demonstrate both my UX and programming skills.
